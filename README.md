# jackbutcher.md

A writing profile distilled from 50,000 tweets.

Voice, structure, rhetorical patterns, themes, and anti-patterns. Everything an AI needs to write in this voice. One file.

## What this is

6 years of writing. 50,000 tweets. Filtered to the 14,000 that performed. Distilled into the patterns that made them work.

Not a course. Not a book. A system file you deploy.

Drop it into Claude, ChatGPT, or any AI tool and generate content in this voice instantly.

## What's inside

- **Voice.** Direct, declarative, every word earns its place.
- **Structure.** Fragments, parallel constructions, inversions, lists that build.
- **Rhetorical moves.** Contrast pairs, reframes, compressed wisdom, deadpan humor.
- **Themes.** Leverage, simplicity, action, consistency, ownership, time, focus.
- **Anti-patterns.** What to never do. The difference between sounding like a person and sounding like a prompt.

## How to use it

Paste the contents of `jackbutcher.md` into any AI conversation as context. Then prompt as usual.

```
[paste jackbutcher.md]

Write a post about compounding.
```

The constraints do the work. Anyone can tell AI to "write punchy." This file tells it exactly which moves to make and which to avoid.

## The 6 rhetorical moves

1. **Contrast pairs.** Renting vs. owning. Theory vs. practice. Two words that reframe everything.
2. **Reframes.** Take what someone is doing and recontextualize it. "If you have time to consume, you have time to produce."
3. **Math.** "+2 vs. x2." Quantify abstract ideas to make them undeniable.
4. **Uncomfortable truths.** Say what people avoid saying. "Moving from theory to practice will cost you status."
5. **Compressed wisdom.** Entire frameworks in two lines. "Step 1. Make yourself useful. Step 2. Make yourself scarce."
6. **Deadpan.** Humor through understatement. "Simply know what's going to 1,000x and buy it before it does."

## Why open source

The writing profile isn't the moat. The person is.

Constraints are taste. Taste is what people pay for. But the best way to prove taste is to give it away and let people see what it produces.
